How It Works

The light spectrum plays an integral part to keeping our brains healthy and active. Blue light helps activate Melanopsin, a trigger for hypothalamic orexin neurons which contribute to destressing and alertness. Orange light in the evening unwinds the brain and prepares it for deep sleep, also known as slow-wave sleep. The Sun-Lite uses a blue light bedside lamp to best make use of this phenomenon.

Full mobile integration for alarm controls and alarm time logging. An app on the user's phone provides alarm time, brightness and colour settings, and can show the user a log and graph of the times when they dismiss the alarm.

Variable lighting settings depending on type of awakening. In the case of a timed alarm, the Sun-Lite will trigger a bright white light, as well as peripheral audio cues. In the case of a natural awakening, sun light will trigger the configurable blue light to raise slowly.

Lightweight and unobtrusive in operation. The Sun-Lite will slot into the lifestyle of most individuals effortlessly and does not require the user to change their schedule.

F.A.Q.

Where should I place the sensor and lamp?

The sensor should be placed in a location which receives daylight, preferably such as a windowsill facing east. The lamp should be placed in a location noticable to the user, such as on a bedside table.

How do I set/reset the alarm?

Dismissal is possible from the lamp unit. There is also a custom mobile interface that is provided to interface with the alarm. From there you can set, dismiss and reset the alarm.

How do I change the time?

The time is automatically synchronised to the server. If your location changes, the clock will update to the current time of your new location.

Sunlight or Sun-Lite?

Natural light is good for you, of course. But Sun-Lite is the product for you if you need that additional boost, or if your bedroom is lacking in natural lighting sources, such as a basement room.
